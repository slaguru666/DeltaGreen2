0 · Briefing handler "Provost" · cover: joint FAA/NWS field team

Situation
0312 local: FAA radar Site ME-07 "Sable Ridge" transmitted a corrupted data loop and went dark. No remote reboot. Deputy Marcus Vance drove up ~0500. No check-in since.
Why DG cares
Pre-crash frames logged a contact not consistent with any aircraft.
Window
Heavy snow tonight, −18°F on high ground, road impassable by dawn. Done before first light.

Mission (priority order)

  1. Erase unnatural data — on-site log drive AND the FAA remote cloud backups.
  2. Recover or terminate Deputy Vance.
  3. Secure the facility.
  4. Cover it up as a catastrophic severe-weather generator failure.
ROE: weapons discreet; no local casualties that can't be blamed on weather or wildlife. If an Agent is compromised by what's on that ridge, the mission comes before the Agent.
Comms: cellular dies at Ridge Road mile 3. Encrypted VHF, callsign OUTLAW-6. If the radios begin to answer, stop transmitting.

1 · The Arrival the compound · heavy snow

Fenced compound: one concrete equipment building, one massive geodesic radar dome above the pines. Gate stands open. Wind, snow, and a hum you feel in your teeth more than hear.

Clues

The chain-link fence is cut from the inside out (anyone examining: no tool marks — the wire is pushed, splayed like a flower).
Deputy's cruiser at the gate: door open, engine dead, interior thick with frost. Forensics/Search service pistol in the footwell, 3 rounds missing, no bullet holes anywhere.
Survival/Track footprints from the driver's seat toward the woods — strides ~8 feet apart. Running, or dragged in great strides. They stop mid-field. Nothing after.

Pacing: let them sweep the compound; the building hum (failing generator) draws them inside. Nothing attacks here. The dread does the work.

2 · The Server Room equipment building · ~40°F · backup power

Emergency lighting; the backup generator hums unsteadily, like something breathing wrong. Racks, a main console — and Dr. Elena Rostova slumped over it.

Rostova is frozen solid in a 40°F room. Eyes wide. Lips torn back from her teeth. No wound a coroner could accept. SAN 0/1 to examine closely.

Clues

Computer Science the data log: the radar tracked no aircraft — it tracked a "geometric shadow" hovering directly over the dish before the crash. Show Handout ⑥.
SIGINT/Science the final transmission: rhythmic oscillating static, a 6-second loop. Listening closely = SAN 0/1D4. Success: a low human voice under the static, whispering the Agents' own names (use their real character names — whisper them). Show Handout ⑦.
Rostova's work notes (priority recovery): the array is "receiving more than it transmits"; readings that "don't make sense in three dimensions."
The corrupted data drive — must be wiped or destroyed. The FAA cloud backup still exists (Computer Science, from here or later).

The ladder in the corner leads up into the dome (Scene 3). When they head for it — or step back outside — run Scene 2.5.

2.5 · The Congregation midpoint · Cold Cauldron Crossing, Ridge Road mile 9

Trigger: the Agents leave the equipment building for the dome, or try to drive back down. Headlights crest the road; under the wind, a dozen human voices humming the six-second loop in unison. A jackknifed log truck blocks the road; vehicles cluster, doors open, engines running.

The snow parts around them like they've been standing there an hour. Old Agnes Corliss stands at the centre in her nightcoat, one hand pressing a dead man's radio handset to her ear. Without turning, she says your point-man's name — first, middle, last — in a voice that is not hers. From the pond shore, two big men in soaked hunting jackets begin to walk up the bank. They have been dead for ten weeks, and they are smiling like they're late for supper.

Balance note (4 agents)

Deliberately unwinnable as a straight shootout: ~34 effective HP of armored Returned, five civilians they must not shoot, and Agnes stripping actions and SAN. The door out is the handset.

Ways to win that aren't massacre

  • Cut the feed: destroy/snatch Elias's handset (called shot −20%, 6 HP, or grapple Agnes). Chorus dies → the Called wake; the Returned stop mid-stride, turn, and walk back into the pond.
  • Deafen her: a flash-bang within 10 m ends the Chorus for good.
  • Names: HUMINT + shouting a Petrie's name = that brother hesitates one full round (once each).
  • Run the gauntlet: ram past the log truck (Drive −20% in snow). The Congregation follows on foot, slowly, humming — they arrive at the compound during the climax if unresolved.

Combat trackers

Aftermath

  • Freed Called remember it as a dream of "the most beautiful voice." All five alive: +1 SAN each Agent.
  • Agnes, freed, keeps asking where Elias went — and once, flat, in the wrong voice: "IT IS ALMOST DONE LEARNING OUR SHAPE."
  • Every civilian death becomes a named problem in the Cover-Up.

3 · The Radar Dome upper catwalk · pitch black · ozone and scorched copper

Total darkness; their lights are the only light. The smell of ozone and burned wiring. And at the central transmitter — Deputy Vance.

Vance is no longer entirely human. Blind — he gouged out his own eyes — yet he rewires the transmitter by feel, fast. His jaw unhinges when he screams: the radar's technical manual, backward. Witnessing him: SAN 1/1D6.
  • He is not trying to kill the Agents — he is trying to finish the broadcast. He attacks only those who interpose.
  • Blindness costs him nothing in the dark; the Agents' lights don't help him or hinder him.
  • Dropping him does not stop the transmitter he has already wired — the countdown to the Broadcast continues (2–3 rounds, Handler's clock).

Combat tracker

4 · The Broadcast climax · the Tear manifests

Whether Vance succeeds or the fight trips it, the dish activates and spins wildly, projecting invisible localized EM. Hair stands on end. The air goes static-charged. The Tear bleeds through the centre of the dish. Show Handout ⑧ now — not before.

The Tear — presence: SAN 1D6/1D20. Bullets pass through and warp uselessly — do not roll damage. It cannot be fought. It can only be starved of signal.

The choice — cut the primary power

  • Blow the backup generator (outside, in the dark, through whatever remains of the night) — Demolitions or improvised; a hazardous run.
  • Manually overload the main transformer inside the dome — lethal electrical shock to whoever does it (Lethality; survivors are badly hurt) + the full 1D6/1D20 exposure at arm's length.

Power dies → the Tear snaps back; the vacuum implodes the dome. Ringing silence. Snow falling through the new hole in the world.

The Truth what's really happening — GM only

The entity is a place adjacent to ours that perceives through resonance — a signal answered by a signal. It has brushed Sable Ridge forever: the spinning compasses, the ridge that "answers," the 1888 hunting party. For most of history it could only whisper to those who whistled back.

In 2016 the FAA parked a high-power transmitter on the one ridge that half-listened, and left it running unattended. Years of steady carrier gave it a stable channel — a door propped wider every night. The phantom returns were it looking back down the beam. The frozen animals and the Petrie brothers were it learning to reach through and feed.

Rostova's servicing tuned the door. The corrupted loop was it learning to speak in our signal — it took her first, then reached down Vance's radio (the one man for miles fluent in signal) and remade him into an instrument: blinded so nothing distracts, wiring the lure to full power.

If the Agents fail: the broadcast completes, the doorway holds, and Sable Ridge becomes a permanent cold spot that swallows the Notch over the winter — a much larger Delta Green problem, later.

2,140 ft ridge ("the Whistle"), 14 gravel miles up Ridge Road from Corliss Notch; winter gate; no cell past mile 3

Unmanned; Sygnal Dynamics contractor visits quarterly; nobody lives within 9 miles

Grid + diesel backup generator (equipment building); main transformer in the dome

Corliss Notch (pop. ~310)

General store (only gas for 40 mi), the Notch Diner, Grange hall, white Congregational church, volunteer FD, and a two-deputy substation Vance runs alone. Logging town losing its young. Strangers are noticed. Locals call the ridge "the Whistle" — it answers, and if you answer back, it draws you into the cold. They don't discuss it with outsiders; they just don't go up after dark.
